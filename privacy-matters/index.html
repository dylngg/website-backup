PRIVACY MATTERS

#privacymatters

Why?

  • If you use the internet, your privacy is most likely not protected. Search engines collect your data, and Ads track you around the web.
  • All of this information collected on the internet is sold to advertisers.
  • Most Importantly, this information is not authorized and can be used against you by a hacker, governments or a malicous people.

This loss of privacy leads to:

  • No social progress -- you wouldn't be able to break the rules in private and being able to express a opinion because everyone would know what you thought and might take action against you.
  • Loss of the abiltity to freely express yourself because you know that other people are watching.

I know what your thinking... It doesn't matter because have nothing to hide.

Well... If you have nothing to hide, then why do you have locks on your bathroom doors? Why do you have curtains? The true answer may be that you don't neccesarilly have "anything to hide". But -- you probably have stuff that you don't feel like showing the world, either. That idea that you don't have to share that stuff is Privacy.

How Your Privacy is being violated:

  • Governments have mass surveillance projects that collect unsuspected user's data:
    • The US has programs like PRISM and XKEYSCORE that collect internet traffic.
    • The UK has used programs like PRISM to collect internet traffic
    • China has the Great Firewall of China which prevents users from freely using all apsects of the web.
    • Here is a great list of mass surveillance projects in the world: Check them out and take action against them.
  • Ads are tracking you
    • Google is collecting data going through their services (search, email, maps, youtube...) to sell to advertisers.
    • Here is a great article at theatlantic.com/: Read about Google's tracking.
  • Hackers are always trying to get your information and use it against you

How to Protect Privacy:

  • Use secure passwords to prevent hackers from hacking your accounts
  • Disable GPS on your phone and read app privlages carefully on your phone (Also read the privacy policies and terms and conditions of apps you are downloading)
  • Encrypt! Use iMessage and other secure apps to keep your messages secure
  • Keep your social media accounts private
  • Use Adblockers that block ad tracking (here's a great one: uBlock Origin(chrome link)
  • Be aware of what the services and products you are using are doing with your data. Most free services often come at the price of your personal data.
